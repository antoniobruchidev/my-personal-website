About me

Hi, I'm Antonio Bruchi, 41 at the time of writing and for the most of my life I've been cheffing. Now I'm currently requalifying as Full Stack Developer.

I'm Italian, born and bred. When I was 14 I decided I wanted to have one year of study abroad so I applied trought AFS, passed many psycologycal evaluations and by the time I was 16 I was accepted and had my year abroad in California. So wonderful, yet hard experience, it'd take a book to tell the tale.

Back in Italy I finished secondary school and started working, a bit as computer repairer, a bit as a chef. This until my late twenties, when after a couple of workplaces I opened a restaurant and run it for two long years. Tired of the very long hours (about 16 per day for 400 in a row), I decided I had to step down and put it up for sale.

After the well deserved rest and when my brother told me I was going to become an uncle, I felt I had to move to U.K to give a hand. So I did and I've been cheffing in Cardiff since 2015. That is until about an year ago when I felt I needed to change. I'm a Code Insitute scholar training to be a Full Stack Developer.

More

I was coming from a bad experience back in Italy, one of those that makes you feel betrayed by the industry you work for (in my case it was hospitality) and I felt I needed to change. I took some time off to explore some ideas and without noticing I found myself completely into solidity and smart contracts, fascinated by the tech behind it, I wanted to know more so I started from the blockchain 101, Bitcoin.

I wanted to mine, or at least to try, so I bought an USB Asic Miner. I had fun setting it up and, trying even to run a bitcoin node to solo mine, didn't have any idea I had to write myself a stratum server because bitcoincore doesn't support GBT solo mining anymore. I had to give up the idea of mining on my own node, so I ended up setting the miner up with one of the not many public solo pools. Anyway, this is the result of my first real encounter with blockchain technology. And yes, I know I'll never going to mine a block with about 200 Gh/s, it was only for educational purpose.

Back to serious things, Smart Contracts: I started studying standards, OpenZeppelin libraries in Solidity. I used remix IDE for my first interaction with testnets (Ethereum and Cronos), I'm making it quite easy, but don't get me wrong, I had and have my fair share of issues! It's a very ambitious project, I would like it to be the first blockchain based company, with its shares and profits released only in the form of ERC20 and ERC1155.
